Key Trends — Cross-Event Analysis
Prime Day is the undisputed revenue king. Both the 2024 and 2025 events hit the highest single-day peaks across all four event types — $100K+ per day — and 2025 expanded to 4 days instead of 2, compounding the effect. 2026 broke the record again at $161.9K on Day 1.
The brand scaled dramatically YoY. The baseline daily run rate grew from ~$30K/day (mid-2024) to ~$45–50K/day (mid-2026) — a ~55% lift in organic velocity before any event boost is applied.
Fall Deal Days punches nearly as hard as Prime Day in peak lift. The 2-day spike in Oct 2024 hit $93–95K/day — nearly matching Prime Day 2024 levels. Fall Deal Days 2025 improved to $110–122K/day.
BFCM behaves differently than the flash events. It's a 12-day extended window with moderate daily lifts (20–46% above baseline) rather than a sharp 2–4× spike. The multi-week spread generates $530K–$636K in total event revenue.
ACOS spikes hardest during BFCM. BFCM 2025 saw ACOS jump to 16.7% during the event vs 11.4% pre-event — the largest ad efficiency hit of any event. Prime Day events show a more moderate 1–2pp increase.
Spring Deal Days has the weakest lift ratio (~10–19% above pre-event baseline) but benefits from a strong seasonal tailwind. The Q1 ACOS environment (1.6% in 2025) is also the most efficient in the calendar year.
Post-Prime Day hangover is real and consistent. After both the 2024 and 2025 Prime Days, sales dropped ~40–50% below pre-event levels for 5–7 days before normalizing. Plan inventory and bids accordingly.
2025 BFCM peak shifted to Cyber Monday. In 2024, Black Friday was the clear single-day peak. In 2025, Cyber Monday (Dec 1) led at $93K — suggesting deal-stacking and late-week purchasing increased YoY.
Prime Day 2024 — Daily Sales Volume
Jul 1 – Aug 7, 2024 · Event: Jul 16–17 · ACOS data not available for this period
Prime Day 2024 — What Happened
+219% lift vs pre-event baseline. The two event days averaged $98K each — more than 3× the $31K pre-event daily average. This was the brand's first major Amazon event at scale.
Sharp cliff on Jul 18. Revenue fell from $95K to $17.7K overnight — an 81% drop. Demand pull-forward was aggressive. It took nearly 2 weeks to recover to pre-event run rates.
Pre-event run rate was already elevated. Jul 1–14 averaged $31K/day — strong organic momentum heading into the event.
Recovery was slower than expected. 14-day post-event average was only $21K/day vs $31K pre — a 30% hangover. Likely a combination of demand pull-forward and seasonal Q3 softness.
Fall Deal Days 2024 — Daily Sales Volume
Sep 24 – Oct 23, 2024 · Event: Oct 8–9 · ACOS data not available for this period
Big Deal Days 2024 — What Happened
Near-Prime Day peak at $93–95K/day. The 2-day spike almost matched Prime Day 2024 in raw dollar terms, despite being a newer, less-marketed event.
+196% lift over pre-event baseline of $31.6K/day — a nearly identical lift ratio to Prime Day 2024.
Soft lead-up. The week of Sep 27 – Oct 6 declined ~10% vs the prior week, likely deal-savvy shoppers holding purchases in anticipation of the event.
Clean recovery. Post-event sales recovered to $26–30K/day within a week — healthier than the Prime Day hangover and consistent with Q4 seasonal ramp.
BFCM 2024 — Daily Sales Volume
Nov 7 – Dec 16, 2024 · Event: Nov 21 – Dec 2 · BF = Black Friday (Nov 29), CM = Cyber Monday (Dec 2) · ACOS data not available for this period
BFCM 2024 — What Happened
Black Friday was the spike, not Cyber Monday. Nov 29 hit $75K — the single-day peak of the event. Cyber Monday (Dec 2) also surged to $55K, but Black Friday led by a meaningful margin.
Mid-event trough on Thanksgiving (Nov 27). Sales dropped to just $28K — below pre-event baseline — suggesting shoppers paused and then returned for Black Friday deals.
Extended event window created ~$532K in total event revenue across 12 days — the highest total-revenue event of 2024.
Post-event drop was significant. Revenue fell sharply after Dec 2 and took nearly 2 weeks to recover, suggesting the BFCM window pulled forward Christmas gift-buying demand.
Spring Deal Days 2025 — Daily Sales Volume & ACOS
Mar 11 – Apr 14, 2025 · Event: Mar 25–31
Spring Deal Days 2025 — What Happened
Weakest lift ratio of all events (+10% over baseline) — but the baseline was already $70K/day, the highest pre-event run rate in the dataset. The brand had scaled dramatically by Q1 2025.
ACOS remained remarkably efficient throughout — pre-event avg 1.6%, event avg 1.8%. The Spring event is the lowest-ACOS window of the full dataset. An ideal time to push aggressive top-of-funnel.
Peak day was Mar 27 at $96K — barely distinguishable from the $90K spike on Mar 16 (pre-event). Strong organic demand partially masked the event lift.
Post-event acceleration, not a hangover. Apr 13–14 saw $100K+ days — Q2 seasonal tailwind built strongly through April.
Prime Day 2025 — Daily Sales Volume & ACOS
Jun 24 – Jul 25, 2025 · Event: Jul 8–11
Prime Day 2025 — What Happened
Jul 8 set the all-time single-day record at the time: $187.6K. A 3.9× spike above the pre-event baseline of $48K/day.
ACOS ticked up +1.9pp during the event (9.4% pre → 11.3% event). Moderate increase relative to the revenue spike — efficient event execution.
4-day event structure kept revenue above $114K every day — total event revenue $555K vs Prime Day 2024's $196K.
Jul 4 dip (Independence Day) visible at just $34.7K — a holiday suppression effect that slightly understated the true pre-event baseline.
Fall Deal Days 2025 — Daily Sales Volume & ACOS
Sep 23 – Oct 22, 2025 · Event: Oct 7–8
Big Deal Days 2025 — What Happened
Record Fall Deal Days peak: $122.8K on Oct 7 — up 29% from Fall Deal Days 2024's peak of $95.3K.
ACOS rose +2.0pp during the event (9.6% pre → 11.6% event) — consistent with Prime Day 2025's pattern. Note ACOS stayed elevated in the 2 weeks post-event as residual deal traffic converted slower.
Two-day event held both days above $110K — strong deal visibility and customer re-engagement on Day 2.
Clean post-event recovery to ~$45K/day within 3–4 days, consistent with the brand's established Q4 run rate.
BFCM 2025 — Daily Sales Volume & ACOS
Nov 6 – Dec 15, 2025 · Event: Nov 20 – Dec 1 · BF = Black Friday (Nov 28), CM = Cyber Monday (Dec 1)
BFCM 2025 — What Happened
Cyber Monday was the peak in 2025 at $93.1K — a reversal from 2024 where Black Friday led. The brand captured more late-week BFCM spend, likely through improved SB/SD retargeting.
ACOS hit its highest point of the dataset during BFCM 2025 — 16.7% event avg vs 11.4% pre-event (+5.3pp). The extended 12-day promotional window with competitive bidding drove the largest ACOS deterioration of any event. Budget planning for 2026 BFCM should account for this.
Total event revenue: $636K across 12 days — a 20% improvement over BFCM 2024's $532K.
Post-event remained elevated through mid-December. Dec 10–14 saw $56–66K days — driven by Christmas gift purchasing. The Q4 flywheel is strengthening.
Spring Deal Days 2026 — Daily Sales Volume & ACOS
Mar 11 – Apr 14, 2026 · Event: Mar 25–31
Spring Deal Days 2026 — What Happened
Brand revenue contracted vs Spring 2025. Pre-event baseline dropped from $70K/day (Mar 2025) to $47K/day (Mar 2026) — a ~33% decline. The most notable YoY softening in the dataset.
ACOS was 12.2% during the event vs 11.4% pre (+0.8pp) — a contained increase, but the absolute ACOS level is now 7–8× higher than Spring 2025's 1.6–1.8%. Reflects the significantly higher ad investment the brand is running at this stage.
Event peak dropped from $96K (2025) to $69K (2026). Worth investigating whether this reflects category competition, listing changes, or a deliberate spend reduction heading into Q2.
Post-event recovered cleanly at $44–63K/day with no hangover. The Apr 3 spike ($62.8K) suggests residual deal-halo traffic after the event window closed.
Prime Day 2026 — Daily Sales Volume & ACOS
Jun 9 – Jun 30, 2026 · Event: Jun 23–26 · Note: Only 4 post-event days available
Prime Day 2026 — What Happened
New all-time record: $161.9K on Jun 23 — a +240% lift over the $41.7K pre-event baseline. The strongest Prime Day performance in the dataset.
ACOS actually improved during the event: 10.0% vs 13.5% pre-event (-3.6pp). An unusual and positive outcome — high-intent Prime Day traffic converted so efficiently that ACOS dropped despite elevated bid competition. Possible sign of strong organic ranking or deal visibility driving conversion without excessive paid support.
All 4 days stayed above $129K — the strongest multi-day consistency across any Prime Day in the dataset. Even Day 4 (Jun 26) held near peak levels.
Post-event data is limited — only 4 days available (through Jun 30). Early signs show recovery to $44–46K/day. Full hangover analysis will require July data.
